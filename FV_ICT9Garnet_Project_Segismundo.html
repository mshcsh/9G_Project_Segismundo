Fire Prevention Tips


Visual Tip Explanation
Enforce a "No Smoking" rule both inside and outside the home One of the leading causes of fire-related fatalities is smoking and cigarette-related fires. These fires frequently start when cigarettes, ashes, or matches are inappropriately dumped and ignite mattresses, bedding, upholstered furniture, or rubbish. You can keep your home and family safe by being aware of what makes smoking fires so dangerous and what you can do to help prevent them.
Unplug all electrical equipment when not in use You may be able to reduce your expenses by unplugging gadgets, and this action will also prolong the life of your possessions. The more things you have plugged in around the house, the more likely it is that a power surge or fire would cause damage.
Keep flammable items out of children's reach and open heat Candles, lighters, and matches are examples of flammable products that need to be stored safely and securely. Extremely flammable substances should be handled, transported, and stored safely to lower the risk of fire and the possibility that they will spark an existing fire.
Put smoke detectors around your house/office Smoke detectors can wake up persons who are asleep before they are burned alive. You may cut your risk of dying in a fire in half by installing and maintaining smoke detectors in your house. Every level of the house should have smoke detectors installed, as well as in each bedroom used for sleeping.
Maintain fire extinguishers in your house/office Extinguishers can protect people and property by extinguishing a minor fire or holding it back until the fire service arrives.
